# Privacy Policy — Task Party

_Last updated: 2026-05-25_

Task Party ("the app", "we", "us") is a study and task-tracking app for students. This policy explains what data the app collects, why it collects it, and the choices you have. By using Task Party you agree to the practices described here.

## Who runs Task Party

Task Party is an independent project published by Christopher (GitHub: `lethalceo`). For any questions about this policy or to make a data request, contact: **conventoc@g.ucla.edu**.

## What we collect

Task Party stores the following categories of data, only as needed to provide the app's features:

- **Account identifiers.** When you sign in with Google, Apple, or email/password, Firebase Authentication creates an account. We store your Firebase user ID, the email address provided by the sign-in provider, and an opaque authentication token. When you sign in anonymously, only the anonymous Firebase user ID is stored.
- **User-supplied content.** Tasks, assignments, exam dates, schedule entries, notes, garden/pet state, friend connections, duel history, leaderboard entries, and the appearance settings you choose. These are stored in your Firebase project so they sync across your devices.
- **Diagnostic data.** Standard Firebase and Expo error logs that record crashes, performance issues, and the device model/OS version that produced them.
- **Apple Sign-In data.** If you use Sign in with Apple, Apple may share your full name and a (real or relay) email address with us. This is stored on your account in Firebase and used solely to label your profile.
- **Google Sign-In data.** If you use Google Sign-In, Google returns your email address and an ID token, which Firebase uses to create your account.

We do **not** collect: precise location, contacts, photos, microphone or camera input, advertising identifiers, browsing history outside the app, or any biometric data.

## How the data is used

- To authenticate you and sync your data across devices.
- To populate social features you opt into (friends, leaderboard, duels).
- To diagnose crashes and improve reliability.
- We do **not** sell your data, share it with advertisers, or use it for targeted advertising.

## Where the data is stored

- **Authentication, database, and crash logs:** Google Firebase (Firestore + Authentication + Crashlytics, if enabled). Subject to Google's privacy practices.
- **App bundles and build infrastructure:** Expo / EAS.
- **On-device only:** appearance preferences, cached sessions (via AsyncStorage).

We retain account data for as long as your account exists. Diagnostic data is retained according to Firebase's default retention window.

## Children

Task Party is intended for users **13 years of age or older**. If you are under 13 (or under the age of consent in your jurisdiction), please do not use the app. If we learn we have collected data from a child under 13, we will delete it.

## Your rights and choices

Regardless of where you live, you can:

- **Access** the data we hold about you.
- **Correct** inaccurate data via the in-app Profile screen.
- **Delete** your account and associated data — email **conventoc@g.ucla.edu** with the subject line "Account deletion" and the email address you signed up with. We will delete the account within 30 days and confirm by email.
- **Withdraw consent** at any time by deleting the app and requesting account deletion.

If you are in the EEA, UK, or California, you also have the rights granted to you under GDPR / UK GDPR / CCPA respectively, including the right to lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority.

## Security

We use the standard transport security (TLS) provided by Firebase and Apple's app sandbox. No system is perfectly secure; we recommend choosing a strong password and not sharing your account.

If we change this policy materially, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where required, notify you in the app. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the new policy.
